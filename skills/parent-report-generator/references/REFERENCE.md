# Parent Report Communication Reference

## Jargon-to-Plain-Language Dictionary

| Educational Term | Parent-Friendly (PT-BR) | Parent-Friendly (EN) |
|-----------------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Proficiente | Demonstra habilidade adequada para a série | Shows grade-level skills |
| Em desenvolvimento | Ainda está construindo essa habilidade | Still building this skill |
| Dominou | Entende muito bem e aplica com segurança | Understands well and applies confidently |
| Avaliação formativa | Verificação rápida durante a aula | Quick check during class |
| Diferenciação | Atividades adaptadas ao ritmo do aluno | Activities adjusted to your child's pace |
| Scaffolding | Apoio gradual até o aluno conseguir sozinho | Step-by-step support until independent |
| Metacognição | Pensar sobre como se aprende | Thinking about how one learns |
| Fluência leitora | Ler com rapidez e compreensão | Reading smoothly and understanding |

## Report Templates

### Brief Update (1 paragraph)
"Olá [nome]! [Aluno] tem mostrado [strength] em [subject]. Especificamente, [example].
Uma área que estamos trabalhando é [growth area]. Em casa, vocês podem ajudar com
[home activity]. Qualquer dúvida, estou à disposição!"

### Full Report Structure
1. **Greeting** (personalized, warm)
2. **Summary** (2-3 sentences: overall picture)
3. **Strengths** (2-3 specific areas with examples)
4. **Growth Areas** (1-2 areas with context and support)
5. **Goals** (what's next)
6. **Home Connection** (2-3 easy activities)
7. **Closing** (encouragement + open door)

### Conference Prep Talking Points
1. Start with a positive anecdote
2. Share specific progress data (show, don't just tell)
3. Identify 1-2 focus areas for next period
4. Collaborate on home-school strategies
5. Answer questions, listen to parent perspective
6. Agree on communication plan going forward

## Home Activity Ideas by Subject

### Reading/Language Arts
- Read together for 10 min daily (take turns)
- Ask "what happened next?" questions about stories
- Play word games (rhymes, categories, 20 questions)
- Have child "teach" you what they learned today

### Math
- Cook together (measuring, fractions, doubling recipes)
- Play board games with counting/strategy
- Spot math in daily life (prices, distances, time)
- Practice telling time on analog clocks

### Science
- Nature walks with observation journal
- Simple kitchen experiments (baking soda + vinegar)
- Weather tracking chart
- "Why?" conversations (encourage questions)

### For Students with Accessibility Needs
- Visual: audiobooks, tactile materials, verbal games
- Hearing: visual games, written communication, sign practice
- Motor: digital tools, voice-to-text, adapted materials
- Cognitive: short sessions, visual schedules, positive reinforcement
